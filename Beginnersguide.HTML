Basic Dictionary:

Price to Earnings ratio is a way to value a company. It's the ratio of company's share price to the company's earnings per share. A high P/E ratio could mean that a company's stock is over-valued, or that investors are expecting high growth in the future. Companies that have no earnings or that are losing money do not have a P/E ratio since there are no earnings.

The price-to-sales ratio shows how much investors are willing to pay per dollar of sales or revenue for a stock. A low ratio could imply the stock is undervalued, while a ratio that is higher than average could indicate that the stock is overvalued. P/S ratio does not account whether the company makes any earnings or it ever will.

Liquidity is the company's ability to easily turn it's assets or investments into cash without affecting it's market price.

EBIT stands for company's earnings before interest and taxes. EBITDA stands for earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization. These indicators are used for measuring company's financial performance and projecting its earnings potential.

Beta indicates how volatile a stock's price is, compared to the overall stock market. A beta higher than 1 shows that the stock is more volatile than the overall market. A beta lower than 1 shows that the stock is less volatile than the overall market.

EPS stands for earnings per share. Earnings per share is a company's net profit divided by the number of common shares it has outstanding. It indicates how much money a company makes for each share of its stock. Because of this, a higher EPS indicates greater value.

PEG stands for price per earnings to growth. The PEG ratio enhances the P/E ratio by adding in expected earnings growth into the calculation. The PEG ratio is considered to be an indicator of a stock's true value, and similar to the P/E ratio, a lower PEG may indicate that a stock is undervalued. The PEG for a given company differs usually from one reported source to another, depending on which growth estimate is used in the calculation.
